MyKlemis App

The MyKlemis App is a working application built for Georgia Tech's on campus food pantry, Klemis Kitchen, as part of the Junior Design class sequence.

Klemis Kitchen aims to serve as an alternate source of meals for students who have special dietary needs or are experiencing financial concerns that cause them to be food insecure.

As of now, Klemis Kitchen currently does not have an application or an inventory management system that both students and admins can use to keep track of what items are available. Furthermore, Klemis Kitchen plans to open new outposts locations across campus and this can be difficult without some sort of app or way to keep organized about the inventory across the locations.

The MyKlemis App helps fill this gap by allowing admins and students to be able to easily view Klemis Kitchenâ€™s entire inventory and also allows admins an easier way to help keep track and make changes to the inventory.

Prototyping & Design

After getting better idea of our user group and how Klemis Kitchen actually operates, we created a paper prototype of the app and user tested it with several peers to gain information on what we can improve.

The result is the figma prototype we created showcasing what we planned to build.



Programming

The final app was programmed using Xamarin with an Amazon Web Services (AWS) backend in addition to Klemis Kitchen's own backend. I helped work on the front end of the app, and helped to program several of the app screens.
Programming was done in five three-week sprints.
