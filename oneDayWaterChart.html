One Day Water Usage Chart

For this project, I created a set of printable 6”x9” cards that act as an activity instruction set for how to make a one day water usage chart.

The question that drove this particular data visualization is: How frequently do I use water from the Chattahoochee River in a single day? My data driven visualization answers that question by presenting my water use data in relationship to the Chattahoochee water use data. It prompts the viewer to make their own data collection and visualization to see their own usage. My answer to the above question is that I tend to use water in spikes around 12pm, 9pm, and 11pm, and it matters to me because I use a surprising amount of water from the Chattahoochee throughout my day and should be more cognizant about how much water I use.

Data was collected by writing every instance of when I used water for a single day. I measured how much water was used (some, estimating through measuring the time ie-- faucets) and kept track of the hour when they were used. I also asked several other people to collect their own data in a similar fashion. The results are the coloful filled spiral charts you see.





All illustrations were drawn by me using Clip Studio Paint.
